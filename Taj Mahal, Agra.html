World Heritage Landmark

Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is one of India's most admired monuments, celebrated for its white marble, symmetry, and lasting cultural importance.

Quick Facts

  • Located on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra
  • Commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632
  • Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983

About the Destination

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is widely regarded as one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian design elements into a single, balanced composition.

Visitors are drawn to its polished marble surfaces, delicate inlay work, and the formal garden layout that frames the monument. The surrounding complex includes a mosque, a guest house, and carefully planned pathways that make the site feel both grand and peaceful.

Today, the Taj Mahal remains a symbol of love and craftsmanship. It attracts travelers from all over the world and continues to represent an important part of India's architectural and cultural heritage.
